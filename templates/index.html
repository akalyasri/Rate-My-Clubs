What your peers say about your college's clubs

WWU Cycling Club

Bellingham, WA

I joined WWU Cycling this year, and it's been a solid experience overall. You just need a bike and helmet to get started, and they’re open to everyone, whether you’re hardcore into racing or just want to go on chill rides.

WSU VR Club

Pullman, WA

The WSU VR Club is honestly pretty fun if you're into virtual reality or just curious about it. They’ve got a good setup with different VR gear, so you get to try out a bunch of games and experiences without needing your own equipment.

UW Chess Club

Seattle, WA

The UW Chess Club could use some improvement. The meetings are consistent, but they can feel a bit disorganized. It’s great that they welcome all levels, but sometimes it’s hard to get a quality game in when people are at such different skill levels.

WSU Cougs in Space

Pullman, WA

Cougs in Space is a really impressive club if you’re interested in hands-on experience with satellite engineering. Only downside is that some tasks are pretty technical, so it may take a bit to get up to speed if you’re newer to engineering.

UCB Biomedical Engineering Society

Berkeley, CA

BMES is a solid club for anyone interested in bioengineering, with plenty of events and networking opportunities throughout the year. However, sometimes it feels like a lot of the activities are geared more toward those with experience in the field.
